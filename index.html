A Dictionary Has 3 Windows, One American and Two French
You gather words but you do not order them

To you, a sentence is meaningless

Reading each word, it becomes impossible to understand a sentence

Reading a sentence is impossible

In a notebook you write the definition of each found word from a pocket dictionary

When referring to the definitions in your notebook, you first look up each word in a pocket dictionary

You read the pocket dictionary with a much larger dictionary open on your lap

Reading has become exhausting

You gather definitions but you do not order them

I say I am cold

You look for the word cold in your notebook but cannot find it

You look for the word missing in your notebook

You look up the word found in your pocket dictionary

I close the window

I go to bed

At work the next day, I make a coffee

On my way home, I wait at the crossing

The car which is meant to stop, doesn’t stop

I am outside the door and you are stood at the window

You are trying to close the window

The window is closed and you are lost

I kiss your cheek and open the window

I am cold, I say

You shut the window and we go to bed

You Did Not Wait to Watch Me Sink
Everything is ok. Here everything feels blue and I am breathing air that has been kept just for me. I am calm and weighted. I have become slow and there are notes ringing in my ears that leave spaces for silence. There is a weight that feels like warmth against your eyelids. I breathe deeply and feel bubbles as gaps. You’re kicking away from me. You’re going very fast and you’re beginning to become very small. I reach out with my arms and feel a tear. A sudden wetness across my back that is so cold it feels hard. I reach out with my arms but get pulled backwards. By this point you’re so small I can’t see you anymore.
I am Sorry for Scaring You
I am burying bulbs of glass.
I am threading lines of copper
Through snakes from the dirt.
I am screaming into a horse’s mouth.

I am two hands filling with venom,
Swollen fingers that fumble
Teeth deeper into the mud and
I am sorry for scaring you.

I am buried in water, blisters
Soft and I tear through
Bubbling skin. I am forcing
Wire through bone to keep
From crumbling to the ground.

I am burying our horse. I am
Placing holes in the casket to
Allow for rot. I am looking for
My teeth in your mouth.

You tell me to leave but my
Knees are now full of lead.
I am screaming as you pull
The snakes from my mouth.

Haiku

Each Morning
After breakfast
You leave.

I wait until the sun rises
To glue
Speckled shards
From your plate.

An egg with no egg
With a hole.

The missing piece
In your stomach
Or between teeth.
Or pulled from your tongue
And flicked across the room.

I place your egg
Into a box
And wash up the pans.
I wash the plates.
I dry my hands on my legs
And I pick glue
From my fingertips.

In our room
I smell your pillow.
I make our bed.
I open the windows.

Later, it is morning.
I crush the egg against your plate
And wait for the sun to rise.
Howling Grief
It is common for humans to respond to trauma by vomiting.

Pulling open her eyes you cry into her mouth.

A child clings to its grandmother, crying into her leg.

The word Mother means urn. The word Father means void.

Someone old, limping through a hallway, supported at the waist by a nurse.

AIDS kills your mother’s best friend.

The dog shits itself multiple times before the family decide to kill it.

People tell you how old the dog was in justification.

After years of living in a hospital, someone dies surrounded by nurses too tired to cry.

You were told the dog died at home.

You were 9 and you would grind your knuckles against the asphalt during lunches.

You were 13 and you pushed thumb tacks into your thigh during classes.

You were 17 and you would cut yourself with a pocket knife that your father gave you for Christmas.

You are now 28 and still struggle to understand that these things never actually helped.

Afterwards, you held their hand, but you wouldn’t kiss them.

Teeth behind cold rubber.

A mother embraces her son, who towers over her, sobbing uncontrollably.

The smell of bent metal burning.

A brother embraces his mother who screams into the asphalt.

A panic attack in waves over years.

Sadness communicated as exhaustion.

It is common for self-destruction to happen in slow motion.

Your yawn is a silent howl.

How do we prepare a child for a lifetime of decomposition?

Pouring salt across a slug.
Three Poems
Each time you lean for your spoon, your fringe falls into your soup.

I’m still trying to peel away the price without ripping it.


Inside each glove, my hands are wrapped with fur

So with my tongue, I lift the lash from the surface of your eye.

I reach for your hand but there’s no room in your fist.


I am vomiting thousands of brass tacks across our bed.

They dance drunkenly as you swing your hammer.

It’s unbearably hot, but we keep the windows closed.

I Will Brush Your Teeth
For you, your mother grew teeth and I am watching

Bright shards push through gum to nipple.

You’re still so small, but huge studded rows sleep in your skull.

Everything is currently hidden but your mother has made

Everything for you. Soon

Everything will be all at once and I am watching

With a finger to chew on.
